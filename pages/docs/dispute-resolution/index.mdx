# Dispute Resolution

Dispute resolution is how Sew Protocol handles disagreements between parties. When a protected transfer is disputed, the protocol provides multiple pathways to reach a binding outcome.

## Quick Overview

- **What**: A mechanism to fairly resolve disagreements about whether funds should be released, refunded, or split
- **Why**: Transfers involve trust, which sometimes breaks down. Dispute resolution ensures funds don't get permanently stuck
- **How**: Multiple escalation levels, from simple to complex, with economic incentives to discourage frivolous appeals

## Key Concepts at a Glance

| Concept | What it does |
|---------|-------------|
| **Appeal Bond** | Appellants post collateral to escalate decisions; failed appeals are forfeited |
| **Resolver Stake** | Resolvers lock capital as collateral; misconduct results in slashing |
| **Escalation Levels** | Disputes can move from standard → senior → external arbitration |
| **Slashing** | Automatic penalties for timeouts or reversals; protects honest resolvers |
| **Freeze Logic** | Resolvers with insufficient bonds are prevented from accepting new disputes |
