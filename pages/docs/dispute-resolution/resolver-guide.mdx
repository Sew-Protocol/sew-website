# Guide for Resolvers

This guide is for operators interested in becoming resolvers—making dispute resolution decisions and earning fees.

## What is a Resolver?

A resolver is an operator who:
- Reviews dispute evidence and circumstances
- Makes binding (or appealable) decisions about fund distribution
- Earns fees for their work
- Faces economic consequences for misconduct (slashing)
- Manages a bond as collateral

Think of it as arbitration-as-a-service, with skin in the game.

## Why Become a Resolver?

### Earnings

Resolvers earn **per-dispute fees**:
- **Fee structure**: Governance-set percentage of dispute value (typically 1-3%)
- **Example**: 2% fee on a $10,000 dispute = $200 revenue
- **Volume**: Scale earnings by handling more disputes

### Reputation & Governance Weight

Strong resolvers:
- Build public track record (all decisions on-chain)
- May receive preferential selection for higher-value disputes
- Could be promoted to **senior resolver** in v2/v3 (higher fees, higher responsibility)
- Earn governance weight or voting power (governance-dependent)

### Economic Incentives (v2/v3)

In versioning with staking:
- **Rewards**: Earn fees consistently for honest decisions
- **Penalties**: Slashing for timeouts or reversals (financial pain for misconduct)
- **Alignment**: Your earnings are tied to being trustworthy

## Capital Requirements

### Version 1 (Single Trusted Resolver)
- **Bond Required**: None
- **Who**: Governance-appointed address (cannot be self-selected)

### Version 2 (Decentralized Resolution)

**Resolver (Level 1):**
- Minimum bond: **$250** (protects disputes up to ~$1,000)
- Recommended bond: **$500** (covers most disputes, improves selection probability)
- Maximum escrow coverage: 4x bond (capped at $2,000)

**Senior Resolver (Level 2):**
- Minimum bond: **$25,000** (insurance layer)
- Recommended bond: **$50,000-$100,000** (mainnet confidence)
- Backs up to ~33 junior resolvers (at M=3, U=0.5)

**Why the gap?** Seniors are the insurance layer. If capitals are too cheap, delegation has no teeth.

### Version 3 (Decentralized)

**Same as v2, plus:**
- **Utilization factor**: Only 50% of senior bond can be reserved for junior appointments
  - E.g., $100k senior bond = $50k available for delegation
  - Forces conservative senior operators
- **Delegation multiplier (M)**: Each junior appointment reserves 3x junior's bond
  - E.g., $500 junior bond = $1,500 reserved coverage
  - Limits junior appointments to ~33 per $100k senior

### How to Obtain Bonds

**Token-based:**
- Stake SEW tokens (protocol's native token)
- Bond value = token amount × token price

**Stable-coin backed:**
- Use USDC/USDT as bond collateral
- Acceptable in launch phase
- Simplifies risk management

**Mixed (Recommended):**
- 80% stable, 20% SEW (protocol-aligned incentive)
- Balances stability with governance alignment

## Getting Started

### Step 1: Register as a Resolver Candidate

```
1. Deploy a resolver contract or EOA
2. Stake the minimum bond (governance approval required)
3. Pass KYC if required (governance-dependent)
4. Governance approves your candidacy
```

**Timeline:** 1-2 weeks (typical governance cycle)

### Step 2: Set Your Parameters

Choose **dispute categories** you'll handle:
- Examples: "Smart Contracts", "IP Rights", "Goods Delivery", "Service Quality"
- Governance updates router to include you in selection pool
- Can specialize or be generalist (affects fee levels)

Choose **fee preference** (within governance-set bounds):
- Higher fees = fewer disputes (selective operator)
- Lower fees = more disputes (volume operator)

### Step 3: Monitor Your Bond

**Ongoing:**
- Decisions are reviewed and potentially appealed
- Honest decisions earn fees
- Reversals or timeouts trigger slashing
- Bond must stay above minimum or face freeze

**Maintain sufficient bond:**
- If bond falls below minimum, you're frozen
- Cannot accept new disputes
- Cannot withdraw funds
- Must replenish to resume

### Step 4: Replenish & Earn

**Earnings cycle:**
1. Handle disputes, earn fees
2. Fees cover your operational costs + profit
3. Use fees to replenish bonds after slashing
4. Scale with more deposits to handle higher-value disputes

**Long-term:**
- Strong track record → promoted to senior (if in v2+)
- Senior role → appoint and curate juniors (v3)
- Curating juniors well → earn higher fees + delegation rewards

## Expected Returns & Risks

### Conservative Operator Profile

**Setup:**
- Initial bond: $500
- Monthly disputes handled: 10-20
- Average dispute value: $1,000
- Fee rate: 2%

**Economics:**
- Monthly fee revenue: $200-$400
- Annual fee revenue: $2,400-$4,800
- ROI on bond: 5-10% annually (modest)

**Risks:**
- 1-2 reversals/timeouts per year (unlikely)
- Slashing loss: ~$50-$150 per incident
- Downside: Net break-even or small profit

### Aggressive Operator Profile

**Setup:**
- Initial bond: $2,000
- Monthly disputes handled: 50-100
- Average dispute value: $5,000
- Fee rate: 2%

**Economics:**
- Monthly fee revenue: $5,000-$10,000
- Annual fee revenue: $60,000-$120,000
- ROI on bond: 30-60% annually (aggressive)

**Risks:**
- More disputes = more reversals statistically
- Slashing loss: $500-$2,000 per incident (1-2 per year)
- Downside: Still profitable, but with variance

### Senior Operator Profile

**Setup:**
- Bond: $50,000
- Appoints 20-30 junior resolvers
- Earns fees + delegation rewards + curating fee

**Economics:**
- Junior dispute fees: Partially shared with seniors
- Curating fee: Governance-set bonus for managing juniors well
- Annual fee revenue: $50,000-$200,000 (scale-dependent)
- ROI on bond: 20-50% annually

**Risks:**
- Juniors make bad decisions → senior's bond is at risk
- Must actively curate and monitor juniors
- Large bond = large slashing exposure (rare, but catastrophic if happens)

## Tips for Success

### Before You Apply

1. **Understand the dispute categories** you'll handle
   - Read the whitepaper and FAQ for each category
   - Join Discord and ask experienced resolvers

2. **Monitor live disputes** (if available in testnet/early stage)
   - Understand decision patterns
   - Learn what makes a decision reversible
   - Build confidence in your judgment

3. **Ensure you can meet timelines**
   - v1/v2: 24-48 hour resolution deadlines
   - Missed deadlines → slashing
   - Only apply if you can respond within hours

4. **Diversify your bond**
   - Don't use all your capital in bonds
   - Keep reserves for fees and other obligations

### While Operating

1. **Document your decisions**
   - Every decision should be justified by evidence
   - On-chain, this is your defense against appeals
   - Example: "Recipient provided 3 evidence files, all verifiable via hash"

2. **Respond quickly to appeals**
   - You'll be notified if your decision is appealed
   - At senior level, you're the reviewer
   - Fast, clear explanations reduce reversals

3. **Monitor your metrics**
   - **Appeal rate**: % of your decisions that are appealed
   - **Reversal rate**: % of appeals that overturn you
   - **Timeout rate**: % of deadlines you miss
   - High metrics trigger freeze; low metrics improve selection probability

4. **Stay engaged with the protocol**
   - Read governance proposals
   - Understand parameter changes (bond requirements, fees, slashing)
   - Participate in feedback (Discord, governance forums)

5. **Plan for slashing**
   - Assume 1-2% annual slashing risk (conservative estimate)
   - Your fees should exceed slashing expectations
   - Example: If slashing is $100/year and fees are $400/year, you net $300/year

### If You're Senior

1. **Curate juniors carefully**
   - Bad junior decisions = senior's bond at risk
   - Actively monitor junior performance
   - Remove unreliable juniors when possible

2. **Set clear expectations**
   - Juniors should know your expectations
   - Provide feedback and training
   - Create incentives for good decisions (e.g., performance bonuses)

3. **Diversify your delegation**
   - Don't rely on one junior resolver
   - Spread appointments across categories
   - Reduces correlation risk if one junior fails

## Governance & Parameter Tuning

Resolvers should be aware of key parameters that affect your business:

| Parameter | Impact | Who Tunes | Frequency |
|-----------|--------|-----------|-----------|
| **Bond requirements** | Affects your capital barrier to entry | Governance | Every 6-12 months |
| **Fee rate** | Affects your per-dispute earnings | Governance | Every 3-6 months |
| **Slashing penalties** | Affects your downside risk | Governance | Every 6-12 months (only if issues found) |
| **Resolution deadlines** | Affects your feasibility to operate | Governance | Rare (only if load issues) |

**Governance voting:** If you're an established resolver, you may have voting rights. Use them to advocate for:
- Fee structures that reward honest operators
- Slashing penalties that deter misconduct without killing profitability
- Bond levels that balance decentralization with accessibility

## Troubleshooting

### "I missed a deadline and got slashed. What now?"

- Your bond is reduced by the slashing amount
- If bond falls below minimum, you're frozen
- **Next step:** Replenish your bond to the minimum, then resume operations
- **Prevention:** Set up alerts for dispute deadlines; batch-review disputes at least daily

### "My decision was appealed and I think I was right. Can I defend myself?"

- At the senior level, you'll have a chance to justify your decision
- Present evidence and reasoning clearly
- Senior reviews both positions and makes final call
- If senior agrees with you, appellant's bond is forfeited (validation for you)

### "What if I want to scale to $50k bond but I don't have that capital?"

- **Use staking pools** (if available): Operators pool bonds and share fees
- **Borrow against expected fees** (if available): Some protocols allow this
- **Partner with seniors** (in v3): Juniors don't need huge bonds; seniors do
- **Build gradually:** Start small ($250-$500), earn fees, replenish bond over time

### "What if the protocol is compromised?"

- Resolvers are subject to **emergency pause** (max 7 days, 3x per cycle)
- During pause, new disputes cannot be opened, but resolution continues
- If a vulnerability is found in slashing logic, governance may retroactively adjust
- **Mitigation:** Diversify your capital; don't put everything in bonds
