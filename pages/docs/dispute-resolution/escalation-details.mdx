# Escalation & Appeals

This page explains how disputes move through multiple levels of review, and what happens at each escalation.

## The Escalation Model

Disputes can escalate when a party disagrees with a decision. The number of escalation levels depends on the resolution version:

### v1 (Single Resolver)

```
Dispute Opened
        ↓
Resolver Decides
        ↓
Decision Final
(Governance can override, but not automatic escalation)
```

**Escalation levels:** 0 (decision is binding immediately)

### v2 (Escalating with Incentives)

```
Dispute Opened
        ↓
Level 1: Resolver Reviews (24h deadline)
        ↓
Decision Made
        ↓
Disagree?
        ↓
Post Appeal Bond
        ↓
Level 2: Senior Reviewer Reviews (48h deadline)
        ↓
Final Decision
(All appeals exhausted)
        ↓
Funds Distributed
```

**Escalation levels:** 1 (resolver → senior)

### v3 (Decentralized)

```
Dispute Opened
        ↓
Resolver Selected (capital-weighted, category-based)
        ↓
Level 1: Resolver Reviews (24h deadline)
        ↓
Decision Made
        ↓
Disagree?
        ↓
Post Appeal Bond
        ↓
Level 2: Senior Reviewer Reviews (48h deadline)
        ↓
Decision Made
        ↓
Disagree?
        ↓
Post Another Appeal Bond
        ↓
Level 3: External Arbitrator (Kleros, etc.)
        ↓
Final Binding Decision
        ↓
Funds Distributed
```

**Escalation levels:** 2 (resolver → senior → external)

## Level 1: Initial Resolver

### Role

The first-level reviewer who makes the **initial decision** on a disputed transfer.

**Responsibilities:**
- Review evidence from both parties
- Consider contract terms and facts
- Make a binding decision (subject to appeal)
- Document reasoning clearly
- Meet 24-hour deadline

### Decision Options

The resolver can decide:

1. **Release to recipient**: Funds go to recipient; sender's claim rejected
2. **Refund to sender**: Funds return to sender; recipient's claim rejected
3. **Split between parties**: Divide funds based on partial agreement (e.g., 60/40)

### Selection (v3 Only)

In v3, the resolver is selected **capital-weighted** by category:

```
Categories: "Smart Contracts", "IP Rights", "Goods Delivery", etc.

When dispute is disputed:
1. Category determined
2. All resolvers in that category ranked by:
   a. Available bond (must be sufficient for dispute value)
   b. Performance history (reversal rate, timeout rate)
   c. Randomization (prevent selection gaming)
3. Top resolver offered the dispute
4. If timeout, next resolver in queue
```

### Economics for Resolver

| Item | Amount |
|------|--------|
| Fee earned | dispute_value × 2% (example) |
| Slashing risk | ~2-5% of bond annually (conservative) |
| Net earnings | Fee - expected slashing |

### Risks for Resolver

| Risk | Trigger | Consequence |
|------|---------|-------------|
| **Timeout** | Missed 24-hour deadline | 2% slashing |
| **Reversal** | Senior overturns decision | 5% slashing |
| **Cascade** | 3+ reversals in epoch | 10% slashing + 7-day freeze |

### Common Mistakes

1. **Not documenting**: Senior can't review your reasoning if you don't provide it
2. **Ignoring evidence**: Making a decision without carefully examining files/hashes
3. **Taking too long**: Procrastinating and missing the 24-hour deadline
4. **Dismissing without reason**: "I reject the claim" with no explanation invites appeal

## Level 2: Senior Reviewer (v2+)

### Role

The second-level reviewer who **re-examines** the first resolver's decision when appealed.

**Responsibilities:**
- Review the original decision and reasoning
- Consider appellant's evidence of error
- Make a new independent decision
- Validate or overturn the original decision
- Document reasoning

### When Escalation Happens

An appeal is filed when:
- Appellant disagrees with Level 1 decision
- Appellant posts an appeal bond (typically 5% of dispute value)
- Timeline: Within 48 hours of Level 1 decision

### Decision Process

```
Senior receives:
├─ Original decision & resolver's reasoning
├─ Original dispute evidence
└─ Appellant's challenge (why they think L1 was wrong)

Senior can:
├─ AGREE with L1 (decision stands)
│   └─ Appeal bond forfeited → insurance pool
│   └─ L1 resolver validated
│
└─ DISAGREE with L1 (decision overturned)
    └─ Appeal bond returned to appellant
    └─ L1 resolver slashed (5%)
    └─ New decision is final (or escalates to L3 if in v3)
```

### Senior Responsibilities

Senior resolvers have **more responsibility** than juniors:

1. **Curate junior resolvers** (v3): Select, train, monitor juniors
2. **Validate decisions**: Ensure juniors are making sound choices
3. **Manage delegation**: Distribute disputes across juniors strategically
4. **Maintain bond**: Senior's bond backs juniors (shared slashing in v3)

### Economics for Senior

| Item | Amount |
|------|--------|
| Fee per appeal review | dispute_value × 3-5% (higher than L1) |
| Delegation reward | Varies (governance-dependent) |
| Performance bonus | For high-quality junior curation |
| Slashing risk | ~5-10% of bond annually (higher due to backing juniors) |

### Risks for Senior

| Risk | Trigger | Consequence |
|------|---------|-------------|
| **Timeout** | Missed 48-hour deadline | 2% slashing |
| **Bad juniors** | Junior's decision overturned by you | Your bond at risk (v3) |
| **Cascade** | Multiple juniors fail | Larger slashing exposure |
| **Insurance failure** | Catastrophic losses | Senior's bond contributes |

## Level 3: External Arbitration (v3 Only)

### Role

Final binding arbitration by an external third party (e.g., Kleros court).

**Responsibilities:**
- Make final, binding decision
- Not subject to further appeal within Sew
- Execute fund distribution
- Protocol enforces outcome

### When Escalation Happens

If the appellant still disagrees after Level 2:
- Post another appeal bond (typically 5% of dispute value)
- Case escalates to external arbitrator
- Timing: Variable (depends on arbitrator's queue)

### External Arbitrators

Possible arbitrators (governance chooses):

1. **Kleros**: Crypto-native court using jury selection
2. **Arbitrum DAO**: Arbitration via Arbitrum governance
3. **Umbraco**: On-chain arbitration protocol
4. **Custom**: Sew Protocol could hire traditional arbitrators

Each arbitrator has:
- Own fee structure
- Own timeline
- Own decision quality guarantees

### Decision at Level 3

```
External Arbitrator receives:
├─ Full case history (L1 decision, L2 review)
├─ All evidence
└─ Detailed briefs from both L1 resolver and appellant

Arbitrator can:
├─ Validate entire L1/L2 chain
│   └─ Original distribution stands
│
├─ Find error at L1 only
│   └─ L1 slashed; L2 validated
│
├─ Find error at L2
│   └─ L2 slashed; case sent back to L1 corrected
│
└─ Find completely different outcome
    └─ All prior decisions reversed; new distribution
```

### Economics for External Arbitrators

- Sew pays arbitrator fee (governance-determined)
- Typically 5-15% of dispute value (higher due to final responsibility)
- Might split with losing party (incentivizes accurate claims)

### Characteristics

| Aspect | Detail |
|--------|--------|
| **Speed** | Slow (1-4 weeks typical) |
| **Cost** | High (5-15% of dispute value) |
| **Quality** | Highest (true independent review) |
| **Finality** | Absolute (outside Sew governance) |

## Full Dispute Flow Example (v2)

### Scenario

```
Amount: $1,000 payment for freelance work
Dispute: Sender claims work was low quality
Resolution mode: v2 (Escalating with incentives)
```

### Timeline

**Day 1, 10:00 AM**
```
Sender files dispute
Evidence submitted:
- Project requirements (contract)
- Delivered work (files)
- Sender's complaints (detailed grievance)

Status: Awaiting Level 1 resolver
```

**Day 1, 11:00 AM**
```
Resolver assigned: Alice (smart contract specialist, $500 bond)
Resolver is notified
Deadline: Day 2, 11:00 AM (24 hours)

Action: Alice reviews evidence
```

**Day 2, 09:00 AM**
```
Alice submits decision:

"The contract specified 'well-documented, tested code'. 
The delivered code has:
- 50 code comments (expected: >100)
- Missing test suite (contract required full coverage)
- Works but doesn't meet specifications

Decision: REFUND to sender

Reasoning: Work didn't meet contract terms."

Alice earns: $1,000 × 2% = $20
```

**Day 2, 10:00 AM**
```
Recipient (Bob) receives notification: Funds will be refunded

Bob disagrees: "Work was tested; sender is wrong about requirements"
Bob decides to appeal

Bob posts appeal bond: $1,000 × 5% = $50

Status: Appeal filed, awaiting Level 2 review
Deadline for Senior: Day 3, 10:00 AM (48 hours)
```

**Day 3, 08:00 AM**
```
Senior assigned: Carol (senior smart contract expert, $50,000 bond)
Carol reviews:

1. Alice's decision: Refund sender
2. Alice's reasoning: Missing tests, insufficient comments
3. Bob's appeal: Claims work was tested (provides test files)
4. Contract language: "Must include test suite"
5. Evidence: Bob's test files attached

Carol's analysis:
- Bob DID include tests (in separate folder)
- Test coverage: 87% (contract: ">50%")
- Comments: Valid criticism (42 comments vs 100+ expected)
- Mixed quality: Tests met spec, docs did not

Carol's decision: SPLIT 50/50
- Sender gets $500 (partial work met specs)
- Recipient gets $500 (tests met specs)

Carol reasoning:
"Tests met contract requirements (>50% coverage).
Comments were light but work was functional.
Partial credit is fair."
```

**Day 3, 09:00 AM**
```
Status: Carol's decision enforced
- Alice (L1 resolver):
  - Originally earned: $20
  - Now: Decision reversed
  - Slashing: 5% of $500 bond = $25
  - Alice's bond: $500 → $475

- Bob (appellant):
  - Posted bond: $50
  - Appeal succeeded: $50 returned (not forfeited)
  - Funds received: $500 (corrected distribution)

- Sender:
  - Funds received: $500 (originally wanted $1,000 refund)
  - Dispute cost: Winning appeal resulted in fair split

- Carol (L2 senior):
  - Decision made: $1,000 × 3% = $30 earned
  - No slashing (her decision stands as final in v2)

Funds distributed. Dispute closed.
```

## Full Dispute Flow Example (v3)

### Scenario (Extended)

```
Same as above, but:
- Resolution mode: v3 (Decentralized)
- Bob decides to appeal Carol's decision at Level 2
```

### Timeline Continues

**Day 3, 10:00 AM**
```
Bob receives notification: Split decision

Bob STILL disagrees: "My work was perfect! I should get 100%"
Bob decides to escalate to Level 3

Bob posts appeal bond: $1,000 × 5% = $50

Status: Escalation to external arbitration
Route: Kleros Court
Typical timeline: 2-4 weeks (depends on jury selection)
```

**Day 5-21**
```
Kleros court assigned
Jury selection: ~7 days
Evidence submission: ~3 days
Jury deliberation: ~5 days

Jury reviews:
- Full contract
- All evidence
- All prior decisions (Alice's, Carol's)
- Bob's and Sender's briefs

Possible outcomes:
1. Validate Carol's split (50/50) → Bob loses appeal bond
2. Award to recipient (100%) → Sender loses, Bob validated
3. Refund to sender (0%) → Both prior decisions wrong
```

**Day 22 (Example)**
```
Kleros jury decision: 70% to recipient, 30% to sender

Reasoning: "Work was well-tested but documentation was weak.
70% credit to recipient for test suite quality, 30% to sender
for legitimate documentation concerns."

Distribution:
- Sender: $300
- Recipient (Bob): $700

Slashing:
- Alice (L1): 5% slashed (reversal) → bond $475
- Carol (L2): 1% slashed (backed junior but her split was wrong)
              → bond $50,000 → $49,500

Bob (appellant):
- Posted $50 bond at L2 appeal (failed, forfeited)
- Posted $50 bond at L3 appeal (partially succeeded)
  → $50 returned (jury found him partially right)

Final state:
- Dispute fully resolved via multi-level review
- All parties received binding outcome
- Honest resolvers (Carol, partly) validated
- Dishonest resolvers (Alice) penalized
- System incentivizes careful decision-making
```

## Key Mechanics

### Appeal Bond Forfeiture

When an appeal fails, who gets the forfeited bond?

```
v2:
- Appeal bond → Insurance pool (system-wide protection)

v3:
- L1 appeal bond → Insurance pool
- L2 appeal bond → Insurance pool
- L3 appeal bond → External arbitrator (payment for service)
```

### Reversal Chain

What if a higher level is reversed by even higher level?

```
Level 1 (Alice): Refund
Level 2 (Carol): Split 50/50
Level 3 (Kleros): Reverse to 30/70

Slashing:
- Alice: 5% (reversed by Carol)
- Carol: 1% (reversed by Kleros, partial responsibility)

Carol's loss is less because Kleros only partially disagreed.
(Governance can tune this)
```

### Timeout Cascades

What if multiple levels timeout?

```
Day 1: Dispute opened
Day 2, 11:00 AM: L1 deadline missed (Alice timed out)
  Action: Alice slashed 2%, case escalates to L2 auto

Day 3, 11:00 AM: L1 resolves (Alice finally submits)
  Status: L1 complete (even though late)

Day 4, 11:00 AM: L2 deadline missed (Carol timed out)
  Action: Carol slashed 2%, case escalates to L3 auto

Day 5: L2 resolves (Carol submits late)
  Status: Both L1 and L2 slashed for timeout
```

## Preventing Frivolous Appeals

The appeal bond system is designed to prevent cheap, baseless escalations:

### Economics of Appealing

```
Dispute value: $1,000
Appeal bond: 5% = $50

If I appeal:
- Cost: $50 (forfeited if I lose)
- Upside: Correct distribution (varies)
- Calculation: Only appeal if EV[correct distribution] > $50

Examples:
- If I'm claiming $1,000 refund but only expect 60% chance of winning:
  EV = $600 - $50 = $550 (rational to appeal)

- If I'm claiming $1,000 refund but only expect 3% chance:
  EV = $30 - $50 = -$20 (not rational to appeal)
```

### Governance Can Tune

If appeals are too frequent → raise appeal bond %  
If appeals are too rare → lower appeal bond %

Governance adjusts based on real data.
