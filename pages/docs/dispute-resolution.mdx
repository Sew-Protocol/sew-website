# Dispute Resolution

> **New to dispute resolution?** Start with [What is Dispute Resolution?](./overview) for an accessible introduction.

Dispute resolution handles disagreements between parties in a protected transfer. When both parties agree on a transfer's terms, everything works smoothly. When they disagree, Sew provides multiple escalation pathways to reach a fair, binding outcome.

## The Basics

### Dispute Lifecycle

1. **Initiation**: Either party opens a dispute with evidence
2. **Pause**: Normal release is suspended; funds held in escrow
3. **Level 1 Review**: Initial resolver examines evidence and decides
4. **Appeal (Optional)**: Dissatisfied party can escalate with an appeal bond
5. **Level 2+ Review**: Higher-level reviewer re-examines the decision
6. **Final Outcome**: After all appeals exhausted, funds are distributed

### Resolution Outcomes

The resolver can decide:

- **Full release to recipient**: Funds go to recipient; sender's claim rejected
- **Full refund to sender**: Funds return to sender; recipient's claim rejected
- **Partial split**: Divide funds based on partial agreement (e.g., 60/40)

## Three Versions: v1, v2, v3

Sew's dispute resolution evolves to add decentralization and economic security:

| Version | Status | Escalation | Economics | Use Case |
|---------|--------|-----------|-----------|----------|
| **v1** | 🟢 Active | None (1 resolver) | No bonding | Launch phase |
| **v2** | 🟡 Built, deferred | 1 level (L1 → L2) | Resolver staking + appeal bonds | Growing volume |
| **v3** | 🔵 Future | 2 levels (L1 → L2 → L3) | Capital-weighted delegation + insurance | Mature, decentralized |

Each version is **backward compatible**: v1 escrows stay v1; new escrows choose their version at creation.

### v1: Single Trusted Resolver

- Governance-appointed resolver
- Fast, simple decisions
- Suitable for bootstrap phase
- **No appeal mechanism** (governance can override)

👉 See [Resolution Modes](./resolution-modes) for details.

### v2: Escalating with Incentives

- Multiple resolvers with economic incentives
- One escalation level (resolver → senior)
- **Staking**: Resolvers post bonds as collateral
- **Slashing**: Penalties for timeouts or wrong decisions
- **Appeal bonds**: Appellants post collateral to escalate

👉 See [Resolution Modes](./resolution-modes) for comparison.

### v3: Decentralized Incentives

- Fully decentralized resolver selection
- Two escalation levels (resolver → senior → external arbitration)
- **Capital-weighted delegation**: Seniors appoint juniors, backed by their bond
- **Three-layer slashing**: Losses distributed across resolver → senior → insurance pool
- **External arbitration**: Final disputes handled by third parties (e.g., Kleros)

👉 See [Resolution Modes](./resolution-modes) for v3 details.

## Key Concepts

### Resolver

An operator who reviews disputes and makes binding (or appealable) decisions.

- Posts a **bond** as collateral
- Earns **fees** for handling disputes
- Faces **slashing** penalties for misconduct
- Can be promoted to **senior** (v2/v3) with higher responsibility

### Appeal Bond

A deposit posted by the appellant to escalate a decision. Example amounts:

- Typical: 5% of dispute value
- If appeal succeeds: Bond is returned to appellant
- If appeal fails: Bond is forfeited to insurance pool

The bond system discourages frivolous escalation.

### Slashing

Automatic penalties applied to resolvers for:

- **Timeout**: Missed deadline (24-48h) → 2% of bond slashed
- **Reversal**: Decision overturned → 5% of bond slashed
- **Severe**: Multiple failures in one week → 10% slashed + 7-day operational freeze

Slashing aligns resolver incentives with honest decisions.

### Freeze Logic

When a resolver's bond falls below the minimum required:

- Cannot accept new disputes
- Cannot withdraw remaining funds
- Must replenish bond to resume operations

This prevents undercapitalized operators from handling high-value disputes.

## Dispute States

| State      | Description                           |
| ---------- | ------------------------------------- |
| `DISPUTED` | Dispute opened, release paused        |
| `PENDING_RESOLUTION` | Awaiting resolver decision (L1) |
| `PENDING_APPEAL` | Appeal window open |
| `IN_APPEAL` | Under senior/higher-level review |
| `RESOLVED` | Final decision made, funds distributed |

## Timelines

### v1 (Single Resolver)

- Dispute → Decision: 24-48 hours
- **Total**: ~1 day

### v2 (Escalating)

- L1 (Resolver): 24 hours
- Appeal period: 48 hours
- L2 (Senior): 48 hours
- **Total**: 3-4 days typical

### v3 (Decentralized)

- L1 (Resolver): 24 hours
- Appeal period: 48 hours
- L2 (Senior): 48 hours
- Appeal period: 48 hours
- L3 (External): Variable (1-4 weeks for Kleros, etc.)
- **Total**: 4-7 days typical (longer with external arbitration)

## Costs

### Resolution Fee

Resolvers earn a governance-set fee:

- Typical: **2-3% of dispute value**
- Example: 2% of $1,000 dispute = $20 to resolver

### Appeal Bond

Appellants post collateral:

- Typical: **5% of dispute value**
- Example: 5% of $1,000 dispute = $50 bond

If appeal succeeds: Bond is returned  
If appeal fails: Bond is forfeited to insurance pool
